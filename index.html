Human Perspectives

Why real accessibility matters

Real reflections on logistical gaps, daily transactional hurdles, and the true meaning of community inclusion across regional panchayats.

“

"Most of the time, my daily life depends on others. Whether it’s buying something or traveling somewhere, I often have to rely on someone to help me.

As a B.Ed graduate, I need to buy books, materials, and other essentials. But arranging everything or going out to get them takes a lot of time… and it’s not always easy. Sometimes, it feels inconvenient and time-consuming.

But I often think… what if there was a simple solution? Something that could help me buy what I need or travel where I want—quickly and easily, without depending on others.

If something like that becomes available, it could save so much time and make life a lot easier for people like me. And I truly feel… it would be really beneficial for all of us."

A

Arya

B.Ed Graduate • Pallikkara Panchayat Resident

“

"During my visits to Kerala, I found it surprisingly difficult to access and coordinate everyday services that are readily available through centralized digital platforms in the United States. Tasks such as arranging online grocery delivery, accessing medical services, booking household assistance, and finding reliable service providers often required significant effort and research.

As someone who has experienced the convenience of integrated digital services in the U.S., I see a tremendous opportunity for Kerala to further embrace this level of accessibility and convenience... A unified platform could greatly improve convenience, efficiency, and quality of life for residents and visitors alike. My experiences highlighted a clear gap in the market."

N

Nilima

Charlotte, North Carolina • International Visitor Perspective

“

"Living in a rural area has made me realize how difficult it can be to access even the most basic services. Something as simple as buying groceries, medicines, or household essentials often requires traveling long distances. In many cases, people have to walk several kilometers just to reach the nearest shop.

The situation becomes even more challenging for elderly residents, women, students, and families who may not have easy access to transportation... Many people simply accept these difficulties as part of daily life because there are very few alternatives available.

By bringing multiple services together on a single platform, ANY has the potential to connect people seamlessly. For communities like Kumbadaje, this is not just about convenience—it is about accessibility, inclusion, and improving quality of life."

D

Deepika

M.Sc. Microbiology • Kumbadaje Panchayat Resident

“

"Although I do not live in a completely remote area, accessing everyday services is still challenging. Food from nearby hotels is sometimes delivered through informal arrangements or unauthorized individuals, but there is no reliable system that people can consistently depend on.

The problem extends beyond food delivery. There is no single platform where residents can access essential services such as transportation, healthcare assistance, home services, grocery delivery, or trusted local providers. Most tasks require multiple phone calls, personal contacts, and unnecessary coordination.

As a working professional, I often find that simple daily needs consume more time and effort than they should. What should be a quick task can become a time-consuming process because services are fragmented and difficult to access.

I believe a platform like ANY could make a meaningful difference by bringing trusted local services together in one place. Instead of relying on informal arrangements or searching for individual providers, people could access the services they need through a single, reliable platform.

For communities like Uduma, this is not just about convenience. It is about creating a dependable system that saves time, improves accessibility, and makes everyday life easier for everyone."

J

Jijila G.

Special Educator • Uduma Panchayat Resident
